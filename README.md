# Color Bar Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** May 26, 2026
**Last updated:** May 26, 2026

This policy explains what information **Color Bar** (the "App," "we," "us," or "our") collects when you use it, how we use that information, and the choices you have.

Color Bar is a one-time-purchase premium puzzle game. There are no ads, no in-app purchases, no subscriptions, no analytics, no accounts, and no backend of our own. The vast majority of your gameplay never leaves your device.

## 2. Information we collect

### 2.1 Stored on your device only

The following data stays on your iPhone or iPad and is **never transmitted to us or to any third party**:

- Your level progress (which endless levels you've unlocked, your best star rating per level)
- Your daily-challenge history and current streak
- Your lifetime stats (total levels played, total drops poured)
- Paintings you create in the 3-star reward mini-game (saved locally as PNG blobs)
- App settings (color-blind mode, reduce motion, tutorial toggle, etc.)
- Diagnostic reports generated by Apple's MetricKit framework (battery, performance, crash logs) — stored locally for your reference; we have no backend to send them to.

We store this information using iOS's standard local-storage mechanisms (SwiftData, UserDefaults). It is included in your encrypted iCloud or iTunes device backups if you have those enabled — that backup is controlled by Apple and your iCloud account, not by us.

### 2.2 Game Center

If you sign in to Apple's **Game Center** service (it prompts on first launch — you can decline or sign in later from iOS Settings), the App will:

- Read your Game Center player ID so it can submit your scores to two leaderboards (Endless and Daily Challenge).
- Submit your scores and unlocked achievements to Apple's Game Center servers.

Game Center is operated by Apple Inc. Their handling of your data is governed by [Apple's Privacy Policy](https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/). We never see your real name, email address, or Apple ID — only your in-game player ID and scores. You can disable Game Center entirely from iOS **Settings → Game Center**.

### 2.3 What we do **not** collect

- We have no advertising SDK. There is no AdMob, no IDFA, no App Tracking Transparency prompt.
- We have no analytics backend. We do not use Firebase Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude, or any other event-tracking service.
- We have no in-app purchases or subscriptions. Color Bar is a one-time-purchase app — Apple handles the App Store transaction; we never see your payment information.
- We have no user accounts or sign-up. There is no email address, password, real name, phone number, or social-graph data tied to the App.
- We do not access your camera, microphone, photos, contacts, calendars, reminders, health data, motion data, or location.
- We do not use cookies, web beacons, or any cross-app or cross-website tracking technologies.

## 3. How we use the information

The information collected as described above is used **only** for these purposes:

- To save your progress, settings, and paintings on your device so the App remembers where you left off.
- To submit your scores to Apple's Game Center leaderboards and unlock achievements (if you've signed in to Game Center).
- To diagnose crashes and performance issues on your own device (via Apple's MetricKit, locally).

We do not sell, rent, share, or trade your personal information for any purpose. We do not build advertising profiles about you. We do not combine the information described above with data from other sources.

---

## 4. Children's privacy

Color Bar is rated for ages 4+ and is designed to be family-friendly. We do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information from children under 13. The App has no advertising SDK, no in-app purchases, no chat, and no user-generated content sharing, so the data-collection surface accessible to children is effectively limited to optional Game Center participation (which Apple gates with its own parental controls).

If you believe a child has used the App and provided personal information without parental consent, please contact us at the email above so we can remove anything affected.

---

## 5. International data transfers

Apple's Game Center is a global service. Information sent to it may be transferred to and processed in countries other than your own, including the United States. Apple maintains its own legal bases for these transfers (Standard Contractual Clauses, the EU-US Data Privacy Framework, etc.); see Apple's privacy policy linked above.

---

## 6. Data retention

- **On your device:** progress and settings persist until you delete the App or clear its data.
- **Game Center:** Apple's standard retention rules apply.
- **No data we control:** there's nothing on our side to retain or delete, because we don't operate a server.

---

## 7. Security

The App uses standard iOS protections — encrypted local storage, sandboxed app container, signed transactions via StoreKit (for the purchase itself), HTTPS-only network requests (only for Game Center). We cannot guarantee absolute security of any information transmitted over the internet, but we follow industry-standard practices to protect what passes through.

## 8. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights under privacy laws like the **EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)**, the **UK GDPR**, the **California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA / CPRA)**, or similar legislation. These can include the right to access, correct, delete, or port your personal information, and the right to opt out of "sale" or "sharing" of personal information.

Because the information we directly handle stays on your device, you can exercise most of these rights yourself: deleting the App removes all on-device data.

For information held by Apple (Game Center), exercise those rights through Apple's channels at <https://privacy.apple.com>.

If you need help or have questions about your rights, contact us at the email above.
